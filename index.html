‚öñÔ∏è

Understanding the Regulatory Impact

The EU forced Microsoft to separate Teams from Office 365/Microsoft 365 to promote competition. This affects ALL customers globally, not just EU organizations. We now need Microsoft 365 E5 ($57/month) + Teams Enterprise ($5.51/month) = $63/month per user. This isn't Microsoft's choice - it's regulatory compliance. The good news: we still save $67,464 annually while getting superior functionality.

üí°

What We're Adding vs. Removing

ADDING: M365 E5 (full Office suite + enterprise security) + Teams Enterprise (collaboration platform). REMOVING: Google Workspace ($131,220/year), current EM+S E5 ($32,669/year), Business Standard ($25,500/year), and Virtru ($6,594/year). Despite the forced dual licensing, we're consolidating from 4 systems to 2 licenses per user, dramatically simplifying administration.

1

The Complete Solution We're Getting

M365 E5 includes: Full Office applications, Exchange Online, SharePoint, OneDrive, Enterprise Mobility + Security, Windows 10/11 licensing, advanced compliance tools, and built-in data protection. Teams Enterprise adds: Advanced meeting features, enterprise calling, compliance recording, and premium collaboration tools. Together, they replace everything we currently have across our 4 separate systems.

2

Why This Still Makes Financial Sense

Even with the regulatory-required dual licensing, we're saving $5,622 monthly ($67,464 annually). We're eliminating: Google Workspace admin complexity, Virtru separate billing, managing multiple Microsoft license types, and vendor relationship complexity. The operational efficiency gains, combined with enhanced security and productivity features, make this migration financially and strategically sound.

3

Administrative Simplification Despite Dual Licensing

While we need two licenses per user due to regulations, both are managed through the same Microsoft 365 admin center. We'll eliminate: Google Admin Console, Virtru management portal, separate support relationships, and complex user provisioning across multiple systems. One vendor, one admin portal, one security model - just with two license components instead of one bundled license.
